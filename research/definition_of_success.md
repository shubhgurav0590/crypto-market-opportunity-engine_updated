# Definition of Success

Success for this system is defined by safety, stability, and long-term usability,
not by extreme profits.

## A successful system:
- Preserves at least 80–90% of user capital during adverse periods.
- Avoids catastrophic drawdowns.
- Reduces trading activity during high-risk conditions.
- Maintains stable behavior across market regimes.
- Helps users stay engaged without emotional stress.

## What success does NOT require:
- Beating the market every year.
- Maximizing short-term returns.
- High trade frequency.
- Constant market participation.

## Long-Term Success Indicators
- Users remain active longer.
- Capital survives longer.
- Fewer forced exits due to losses.
- Trust increases over time.

A system that helps users survive is considered successful,
even if returns are moderate.
