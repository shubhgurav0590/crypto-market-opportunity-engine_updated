# Research Assumptions

This research is based on the following assumptions. These assumptions are stated
explicitly to maintain transparency and avoid misleading conclusions.

## Market Assumptions
- Historical price data reasonably reflects market behavior.
- Bitcoin (BTC) is liquid enough to allow simulated execution without extreme slippage.
- Market regimes repeat in recognizable patterns, though not perfectly.

## Data Assumptions
- Price and volume data are accurate and correctly timestamped.
- Transaction costs and slippage can be reasonably approximated.
- No future information is used in decision-making (no data leakage).

## Model Assumptions
- Machine learning models capture patterns, not causes.
- Model confidence is imperfect and must be treated cautiously.
- No model is assumed to work in all market conditions.

## User Assumptions
- Users accept conservative risk limits.
- Users may act emotionally, which requires system safeguards.
- Users may override warnings, but system-level protection remains active.

## System Assumptions
- System failures are possible and must be detected early.
- “Not trading” is an acceptable and sometimes optimal decision.
- Capital protection is prioritized over profit maximization.

These assumptions define the boundaries within which the research conclusions hold.
