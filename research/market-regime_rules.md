1. What is a market regime?

Explain in 3–4 lines:

A market regime describes the general behavior of the market over a period of time, such as trending, sideways, or highly volatile.

2. Why regime detection matters

Explain simply:

One strategy cannot work everywhere

Risk is different in each regime

Small investors suffer most when regimes change suddenly

3. Regime categories (keep it simple)

Example:

Trending Market

Price moves consistently up or down

Volatility is moderate

Indicators agree

Sideways / Range-bound Market

Price moves within a range

No clear direction

Many false signals

High-Risk / Volatile Market

Sudden large price moves

Volatility spikes

Liquidity unstable

⚠️ Do NOT overcomplicate this early.

4. How regimes are identified (rule-based first)

Explain what signals you look at, not formulas:

Price trend strength

Volatility level

Speed of price changes

Consistency of direction

Important line to include:

“Only past data is used. No future information is allowed.”

This avoids data leakage accusations.

5. What the system does in each regime

Very important for impact:

Trending → normal or cautious trading

Sideways → reduced trading

High-risk → stop or heavily restrict trading

This shows capital protection logic.

6. Limitations (honesty = credibility)

Say clearly:

Regimes are approximations

Boundaries are not perfect

Regime detection can lag during sudden changes

Reviewers LOVE this honesty.


Market regimes are used to adapt risk, not to predict the future.
